About Dagmars Artistry

Dagmar Glemme’s paintings, lithographs and sculptures in ceramic, glass, bronze and stone have been shown in over 300 exhibitions world‐wide. Dagmar's many pieces of public art include a painting for peace displayed at the UN in New York, the sculpture "Tree of Knowledge" outside Art Clinic in Jönköping and four large murals in the Church of Resurrection in Fort de France, Matinique. In recent years Dagmar Glemme has designed glass sculptures in the furnaces at Berongo Fine Arts in Murano/Venice, Schott/Jena in Brazil, Bergdala Studioglas and Studio Micke Johansson, Örsjö. Dagmar Glemme´s largest public work to date is "Weg der Steine" (“Avenue of the Stones”), where wenty‐one large stone scuptures (2‐3 meters tall) adorn the streets in the heart of Uelzen, Germany. Dagmar is also the current artist chosen to paint the windows of a two‐story tall advent calendar displayed on the facade of the old Town Hall of Uelzen each December. Dagmar Glemme also produces art and music films, for example “The Six Elements” (2008) and an artistic portrait of the Swedish province of Halland, interpreted through the eyes of local musicians, artists and writers. She has designed several CD art covers and in 2012 she wrote and illustrated her first children's book, named "Colorful Lies". Dagmar Glemme is a member of KRO (The Swedish Artists´ National Organization) and NVSK.
